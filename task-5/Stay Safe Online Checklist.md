STAY SAFE ONLINE CHECKLIST:



1. Never Share Your UPI PIN/OTP: Remember that a UPI PIN is only required to send money, never to receive it. If someone asks you to enter your PIN to "receive a prize" or "verify a refund," it is a scam.

2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Use 2FA on all sensitive accounts (Email, Social Media, Banking). As of 2026, many Indian banks have shifted to mandatory multi-factor authentication, including biometrics or dynamic OTPs.

3. Verify UPI IDs Before Paying: Always double-check the recipient's name and Virtual Payment Address (VPA) before hitting 'send.' Scammers often use names that look like official brands or government departments

4. Use Strong, Unique Passwords: Avoid using the same password across multiple sites. Use a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols—or better yet, a trusted password manager.

5. Keep Software Updated: Regularly update your phone’s OS and banking apps. These updates often contain "security patches" that fix vulnerabilities hackers use to gain access.

6. Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks: Never log into your bank or make payments while connected to free public Wi-Fi. Use your mobile data or a trusted VPN to encrypt your connection.

7. Be Skeptical of "Urgent" Messages: Phishing attacks rely on creating panic (e.g., "Your account will be blocked in 2 hours!"). Take a breath and verify the claim through the official app or website—never through the link in the message.

8. Audit App Permissions: Periodically check which apps have access to your contacts, SMS, and location. Delete any apps you no longer use to reduce your "digital footprint.
