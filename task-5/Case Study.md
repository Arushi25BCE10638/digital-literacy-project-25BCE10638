1. THE SCENARIO:

In this common phishing scheme, a user receives a high-pressure SMS or email masquerading as a reputable bank or a popular digital payment service.
The message warns that the user's account will be permanently blocked within two hours unless they verify their KYC (Know Your Customer) details immediately.
Driven by the fear of losing access to their funds, the user clicks a provided link that leads to a sophisticated, near-identical replica of their bank’s actual login portal.



2. HOW IT HAPPENS:

The mechanics rely on social engineering and technical deception:

(a) Deceptive Links: The URL might look legitimate (e.g., www.yourbank-kyc.com instead of www.yourbank.com).

(b) Data Harvesting: Once on the fake site, the user enters their username, password, and mobile number.

(c) OTP Interception: The fraudulent site prompts for an OTP (One-Time Password). When the user enters the code sent to their phone, the attacker—who is mirroring these actions on the real banking site in real-time—uses that OTP to authorize an unauthorized fund transfer or change the account’s security settings.



3. THE CONSEQUENCES:

The immediate impact is usually the total drainage of the linked savings account.
Beyond the direct financial loss, victims often face a grueling recovery process involving police reports (Cyber Cell) and bank disputes, which rarely result in a full refund if the OTP was voluntarily shared.
Long-term consequences include identity theft, as the attackers now possess enough personal data to open fraudulent lines of credit in the victim's name.
