Researching these modern fraud tactics is a sobering reminder of how easily our psychological triggers—like urgency and fear—can be exploited by technology.
It is surprising how scammers have moved beyond simple malware to sophisticated real-time mirroring, where they act as a man-in-the-middle to intercept OTPs as they are generated.
The fact that a single lapse in judgment during a 30-second phone call can bypass years of sophisticated banking encryption is a startling reality of the digital age.



One personal habit I will change is my tendency to trust caller ID names and SMS headers.
In the past, if a message appeared in a thread labeled "Bank Info" or a caller showed up as a "Support Agent," I would lower my guard.
Going forward, I will adopt a "Zero Trust" policy: if an unsolicited contact asks for any action involving a link or a PIN, I will hang up and manually type the official website address into my browser instead.
Growing comfortable with that extra ten seconds of effort is a small price for total financial security.
